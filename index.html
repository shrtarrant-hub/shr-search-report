The year, charted

A winter slide, then a spring–summer turnaround

The work

What was actually done (June 11 – July 8)

Dozens of live fixes across six working stretches — every change verified on the live site afterward, and 27 locked regression checks now re-test all past fixes after every new change so improvements can’t silently break.

  1. June 11–13 · Technical deep-clean

    Every crawl error and warning fixed — site health to 100/100

    Full audit, then fixes across ~140 URLs: root-caused duplicate structured-data (schema) layers, built the kitchen hub + city pages, fixed bathroom content that had leaked onto all 12 kitchen city pages, standardized pricing sitewide.

  2. June 16–19 · Trust & accuracy pass

    19-agent audit → 69 verified findings → fixes shipped

    Clickable phone number in the footer, private tax IDs stripped from public code, service area corrected to Tarrant/Parker counties, About page rebuilt with real testimonials, llms.txt added for AI search engines, and inaccurate “licensed general contractor” wording removed (Texas doesn’t license GCs — the site now says exactly what’s true: city-registered and insured, since 2001).

  3. June 18 · The invisible revenue bug

    Found & fixed a silent contact-form outage

    A captcha plugin combined with a caching feature had been silently blocking every logged-out/mobile quote submission for ~6 days. Replaced with an invisible server-side spam filter — later verified: 0 of 12 real leads blocked, 17 of 18 spam caught, plus a backup copy of every lead.

  4. June 27–28 · Content integrity rebuild

    17 blog posts rewritten from scratch; geography fixed on ~43 pages

    Old imported posts contained fabricated case studies, fake testimonials, and images hot-linked from competitors — all replaced with accurate, owner-voice, Texas-specific content. Removed stray “Dallas/DFW” targeting so the site is purely Tarrant/Parker. 14 page titles rewritten keyword-first. Crash-proofed robots.txt.

  5. June 29 – July 3 · Off-site push

    Spam-link cleanup, outreach, and conversion pass

    A ~300-domain spam-link flood was hitting the site; a 308-domain disavow file was built and uploaded to Google (verified). 17 link-outreach emails sent to chambers, associations and local media; a Trusted Partners page went live; Google Business Profile access recovered and Search Console reconnected. On-site: mobile sticky call bar and a trust strip + quote form added to both money pages.

  6. July 7–8 · Lead-capture hardening

    Quote form now takes 5 photos × 20 MB — and a photo-loss bug is dead

    Customers can attach real project photos; a pre-existing bug that silently discarded uploads was fixed, photos are now stored and linked in every lead email. Homepage process section rebuilt on the new gold design tokens; stray escaped characters cleaned off 4 pages including the quote form; business-listing addresses audited (fixes staged for two directories still showing an old address).

How to read these numbers (the honest part)

  • Estimates are labeled estimates. Ahrefs “traffic” is a model; in the months where both existed, it ran roughly 3× above real Search Console clicks for this site. Trust its direction, not its level.
  • There’s a real-data gap. Search Console’s feed broke April 6 and was reconnected July 3 — real May/June clicks weren’t available yet when this was written, and April is partial.
  • Timing vs. credit. The ranking recovery began in May, before the hands-on work started June 11. The June–July gains (kitchen terms to #1–3, bathroom onto page 1, top-3 count 19 → 21, best estimated-traffic months of the year) are consistent with the work — but one site’s data can’t prove cause.
  • Authority rose before the engagement. DR 4.2 → 19 happened between July 2025 and March 2026 and has been flat since. Recent “new links” were mostly a spam flood — now disavowed, which protects the site but was claimed as no ranking gain.
  • Still weak, on purpose in this report: the local map pack is flat (~2% visibility across 10 weekly grid scans), reviews are stalled at 120, and the homepage still earns ~95% of all search traffic while the kitchen/bathroom pages don’t rank on their own yet — that’s the biggest untapped lever for the next stretch.
